# MAHADUM.360 Product Context

## Register

Brand.

## Platform

Web-first responsive product: a public marketing site and authenticated React SPA backed by a Laravel API.

## Users

- Primary buyers: Nigerian and African diaspora parents who want their children to speak a family language and connect with relatives and culture.
- Primary learners: children and adults beginning or rebuilding Yorùbá, Igbo, Hausa, or Nigerian Pidgin.
- Institutional buyers: Nigerian schools, head teachers, and school administrators who need curriculum, teacher tools, rosters, reporting, and predictable seat pricing.
- Supporting users: parents, teachers, supervisors, and content owners who guide, review, and approve learner activity.

## Product Purpose

MAHADUM.360 helps families restore a language that may be slipping between generations, while giving schools a practical system for teaching Nigerian languages. Learning is joyful, culturally specific, low-bandwidth aware, and complete on the free plan.

The canonical promise is: “Learn the language. Live the culture. Connect the generations.”

## Positioning

MAHADUM.360 is not a generic language app with African languages added as a category. It is a Nigerian-language learning platform built around family participation, native voices, living culture, child-safe identity, and real school operations.

The memorable marketing idea is: “Hear your child say it in your language.”

The primary conversion is a free family account with no card. The strongest secondary conversion is trying a real lesson without signing up. School visitors should be able to understand the offer, inspect pricing bands, and request a quote.

## Belief Ladder

1. Losing everyday access to a family language is painful, but reversible.
2. A five-minute lesson can feel joyful and culturally real, not academic or generic.
3. The child can begin from zero, even if they were born abroad and speak only English.
4. A parent participates through progress, review, chores, coins, and approval rather than merely paying.
5. Child accounts, consent, payments, and weak connections are handled deliberately.
6. Every learning lesson remains available for free; payment adds convenience, not access.

## Proof Available Today

- A playable three-round lesson on the landing page.
- A working parent-approval demonstration that releases coins only after approval.
- Transparent naira pricing for free, individual, family, and school plans.
- Documented child-profile architecture: under-13 learners do not need login credentials.
- Curriculum, roster, assignment, reporting, and billing capabilities for schools.

Do not invent user counts, testimonials, ratings, efficacy claims, partner logos, or adoption metrics until they can be verified.

## Brand Personality

Joyful, rooted, warm, capable, respectful, and contemporary. The product should feel as delightful as a leading global children’s learning app while remaining unmistakably Nigerian through people, language, stories, and context—not through decorative stereotypes.

The master visual relationship is bright sky and white surfaces, confident cobalt/chore blue, energetic orange, deep navy, and the rainbow wordmark. Gold is reserved for coins and rewards.

## Anti-References

- Generic safari, map-of-Africa, Ankara-pattern, or “tribal” decoration used as a shortcut for culture.
- A generic SaaS page made from interchangeable floating cards and empty metrics.
- Fear-led or guilt-led diaspora copy.
- A dark navy-and-gold memorial, luxury, or ceremonial aesthetic as the primary children’s brand.
- A clone of Duolingo’s mascot or interaction language.
- A generic Spanish-learning interface with Nigerian language names swapped in.
- Fabricated testimonials, school logos, usage numbers, ratings, or effectiveness percentages.
- Excessive rounded containers, tiny uppercase labels, or motion that delays access to content.

## Design Principles

1. Let visitors experience learning before registration.
2. Lead with the family outcome, then explain product mechanics.
3. Show culture through consistent people, correct language, stories, and real situations.
4. Make parent participation and school infrastructure visible; these are strategic differentiators.
5. Keep conversion actions visible early on mobile and repeat them only at meaningful decision points.
6. Use motion to clarify state changes and reward participation; all content must remain available without animation or JavaScript-driven reveal.
7. Maintain WCAG 2.1 AA contrast, semantic structure, keyboard access, reduced-motion support, and at least 44px interaction targets.
8. Preserve diacritics and language names accurately, including Yorùbá.
