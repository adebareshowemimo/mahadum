# MAHADUM.360 Landing Variants Strategy

## Why Three Versions

The three routes are not cosmetic A/B themes. Each one answers a different strategic question with its own promise, narrative order, interaction, and image composition. They use the same master brand and verified product capabilities.

## Competitive Synthesis

### What the category leaders own

- **Duolingo:** one clear action, habit formation, short lessons, immediate feedback, rewards, and a strong character memory structure.
- **Lingokids:** entertainment-first positioning, a scalable character world, bold child-facing energy, and parent trust.
- **Dinolingo:** concrete activity breadth, age clarity, up to six children, parent reports, safety, school plans, and substantial verified proof.
- **Koyo:** emotionally precise heritage-language copy and the feeling of using the correct phrase with family, not merely translating it.
- **Dandali:** stories as the product, native-speaker specificity, a deliberate refusal of pan-African blur, and intimate parent-facing narrative.
- **PlayNative:** the closest direct child/African-language offer, combining topic vocabulary, story-based learning, games, and identity-led family copy.
- **AfriRoots, IgboLearn, Alingo, Ningo Africa:** culture-first lessons, native audio, diaspora identity, gamification, dictionaries, community, or schools—but usually only a subset of the full family-school operating system.

### MAHADUM.360’s defendable territory

MAHADUM.360 connects four layers competitors usually separate:

1. **The child learns:** bite-sized lessons, listening, speaking, quizzes, feedback, XP, hearts, badges, leagues, stories, proverbs, festivals, and competitions.
2. **The family participates:** up to six learner profiles, under-13 profiles without logins, progress, speaking review, parent PIN, chores, coins, wallet, and approval before reward release.
3. **Culture supplies context:** tone marks, native voices, greetings for elders, stories, songs, festivals, and the meaning behind when a phrase is used.
4. **The school can operate it:** organization tenancy, CSV rosters, seats, classes, assignments, grading, learner analytics, invoices, clubs, referrals, and competition entry.

The commercial differentiator is equally important: the complete learning path remains free. Paid plans add convenience and family economy features; they do not sell the language back to the learner.

## V1 — Family Story

**Route:** `/v1`

**Primary audience:** diaspora parents and multi-generational families.

**Physical scene:** a child at home using a new greeting while family joins across a video call in warm late-afternoon light.

**Core promise:** “One hello can bring the whole family closer.”

### Narrative

1. Begin with the desired family moment, not a feature list.
2. Let visitors choose a language and immediately hear/see the first greeting.
3. Show culture as the reason a word matters.
4. Demonstrate the parent’s active role through approval and coins.
5. Close on the complete-free learning promise.

### Image logic

The V1 hero was generated with pale-sky negative space on the left specifically for the headline. The characters, tablet, and eye lines occupy the right side, so copy and image form one conversation rather than two adjacent boxes.

## V2 — Culture World

**Route:** `/v2`

**Primary audience:** children first, with parents as the conversion buyer.

**Physical scene:** Amara entering a bright Nigerian market mission where greetings, counting, objects, music, and people create the lesson.

**Core promise:** “Every new word opens a bigger world.”

### Narrative

1. Lead with the child’s experience: language opens actions and places.
2. Let visitors switch among a market mission, story night, and class challenge.
3. Put a real playable lesson at the centre of the page.
4. Reassure the parent with identity, approvals, and progress controls.
5. Close with a confident ₦0 entry point.

### Image logic

The hero reserves cobalt sky/awning space for white copy while Amara’s gesture pulls the eye from the promise into the market. The interactive adventure selector changes both image and content so the visual is part of the explanation.

## V3 — Whole Community

**Route:** `/v3`

**Primary audience:** school decision-makers, parents seeking structure, and teachers.

**Physical scene:** a classroom opening directly into a school courtyard where family joins the same Language & Culture learning moment.

**Core promise:** “Keep the language moving everywhere a child learns.”

### Narrative

1. Present one system spanning home and school.
2. Let families, schools, and teachers inspect their own product view.
3. Explain the learning loop from lesson to practice, adult feedback, and celebration.
4. Make school pricing tangible with an interactive seat calculator.
5. End with clubs, family stories, and competitions as the community layer.

### Image logic

The left side of the hero is an architectural navy field designed for white copy. It opens physically into the classroom and courtyard, making the layout itself communicate “one connected system.”

## Shared Motion System

All five versions use motion as product explanation rather than decoration:

- hero copy and imagery enter as one composed moment;
- language changes animate the new word, not the whole page;
- tabs transition the related image and content together;
- V1’s approval releases coins, V2’s words float into the cultural world, and V3’s nodes carry a signal from home to lesson to school;
- V4 animates a spoken waveform and daily language journey;
- V5 animates platform capabilities and the handoff from curriculum to reporting.

Animations use transform, opacity, clip-path and bounded background movement. `prefers-reduced-motion` removes all non-essential motion while leaving every feature and piece of content visible.

## V4 — Living Language

**Route:** `/v4`

**Primary audience:** diaspora families that need a credible bridge from a first word to everyday use.

**Physical scene:** Amara and Iya use language naturally while preparing puff-puff in a bright family kitchen.

**Core promise:** “Hear it at breakfast. Say it by bedtime.”

### Narrative

1. Start in a real family activity rather than inside an app screen.
2. Let one phrase move through breakfast, practice, story time, and a family call.
3. Show the complete listen–speak–review–use loop.
4. Explain language depth through pronunciation, cultural context, and accurate diacritics.
5. Demonstrate parent review, child-safe identity, low-bandwidth support, and the complete-free promise.

### Image logic

The kitchen hero reserves a decisive cobalt field for copy while the characters complete a tangible activity on the right. The supporting speaking-review image places the family interaction first and the interface second, reinforcing that the product exists to create conversation.

## V5 — Learning Ecosystem

**Route:** `/v5`

**Primary audience:** school leaders, education partners, and families evaluating the breadth of the platform.

**Physical scene:** a Language and Culture festival makes the platform’s final outcome visible: children confidently speaking in front of teachers and family.

**Core promise:** “Build the place where a language keeps growing.”

### Narrative

1. Lead with the community outcome rather than an administrative dashboard.
2. Let visitors inspect learner, family, teacher, and school roles.
3. Show the five operational handoffs from authoring to reporting.
4. Connect speaking review and analytics to real teaching activity.
5. Carry learning beyond the screen through family stories, clubs, and competitions.
6. Close with free family entry and transparent school pricing paths.

### Image logic

The festival hero uses a deep-navy school architecture field for the promise and holds the performance to the right. The supporting classroom image keeps Amara practising in the same frame as the teacher’s progress view, making the relationship between human teaching and operations explicit.
